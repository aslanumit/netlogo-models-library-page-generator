Invasive Plants

Run on NetLogoWeb

WHAT IS IT?

This is a model showing how invasive plants spread. The simulation works at a timescale of 1 year per tick. Each patch represents a farm. When one becomes too infested, it may be subjected to treatment with weed killer, which will kill some percentage of invasive plants in the farm. To simulate the effect of runoff, a smaller portion of invasive plants in neighboring farms will also be killed.

HOW IT WORKS

Each patch is assigned a number representing the level of infestation. The number is capped at 100, which represents the highest possible level of infestation.

For each patch that has a positive level of infestation, there is a probability that it generates some seeds at each tick. The amount of seeds generated is controlled by the variable seed-production-rate and the level of infestation of the patch. Each seed in the model is meant to represent multiple seeds in the real world, which is why the numbers of generated seeds are relatively small. These seeds are moved away from the patch by a random distance between 0 and spread-distance.

If a seed lands on a patch, the model generates a random number between 0 and 100. If the generated number is larger than the resistance, then the seed germinates, increasing the level of infestation on that patch. Seeds are consumed even if they fail to germinate. The level of resistance is visualized as a gray square laid on top of the patch. The bigger the size of the square, the higher the level of resistance.

If a patch has a level of infestation greater than weed-killer-threshold, it becomes eligible for treatment with weed killer. At each tick, a number of eligible patches are selected for treatment. If a patch is selected, its infestation level will be reduced by weed-killer-effectiveness. The neighboring patches will also have their infestation level reduced by half of weed-killer-effectiveness. This is meant to simulate the effect of weed killer runoff.

The level of infestation in each patch does not decrease on its own. This is because each patch represents a farm, which is an environment that is very supportive of plant growth. Because of this, invasive plants, which tend to be hardier than crops, would generally not die off without outside interventions.

HOW TO USE IT

Use the initial-density slider to choose the initial amount of infested patches. It could be a good idea to set this to 0 if you plan to manually draw a custom pattern of infested patch. Use the initial-resistance-shape chooser to pick a resistance pattern to use. If you prefer to draw a custom pattern, set this to none. Press setup to initialize the model.

To draw a custom pattern of initial infestation, set draw-mode to infestation and press draw. Use the draw-value slider to set the desired infestation level. Click and drag on the view to set the infestation level of the patch under your cursor. If you need to clear the view, press the clear button. You can also draw a custom resistance pattern by setting draw-mode to resistance and following the same process.

Press the go button to start the simulation. Alternatively, you can step through the simulation on a tick-by-tick basis by ensuring that the go button is not depressed and clicking go-once.

Look at the aggregate infestation plot to see how the aggregate level of infestation changes over time. Look at the infestation levels plot to see the distribution of infestation levels across the patches.

The following are descriptions of what the control widgets do:

  • The initial-density slider controls what proportion of the patches are initially made infested.
  • The initial-resistance-shape chooser can be used to put a pattern of resistant patches on the screen.
  • The draw-mode chooser can be used to put the model into drawing modes in which the level of infestation or resistance can be set by drawing on the view.
  • The draw-value slider controls the value the patch under the cursor is set to in drawing modes.
  • The show-seeds? switch controls whether the seeds are displayed.
  • The weed-killer-rate slider controls how many patches are treated with weed killer at each tick.
  • The weed-killer-threshold slider controls the level of infestation at which a patch becomes eligible to be selected for treatment with weed killer.
  • The weed-killer-effectiveness controls how much infestation is reduced when the weed killer is applied.
  • The seed-production-rate controls how many seeds can be generated from an infested patch. The actual number of seeds is also influenced by the level of infestation and the amount of randomness.
  • The spread-distance controls the maximum distance away from the parent patch that seeds can travel to.

THINGS TO NOTICE

Look at the overall shape of infested patches. How does it change over time?

The plot widget on the right shows the aggregate level of infestation. How does the shape of the plot change as you adjust different parameters?

In the initial configuration, the aggregate level of infestation oscillates at the beginning of the simulation. Why does this happen?

THINGS TO TRY

Disable weed killer applications by setting weed-killer-rate and try setting seed-production-rate to a very low value and spread-distance to a very high value. Take note of how the shape of infected patches changes over time. After that, set seed-production-rate to a very high value and spread-distance to a very low value. How do the resulting shapes compare?

EXTENDING THE MODEL

Try adding other factors that can affect how seeds spread. For example, you can simulate winds by making it more likely for seeds to move further in the direction of the wind.

You can also try making it possible to choose a different strategy for targeting infested patches. For example, selecting a patch and then picking a number of closest patches that have a level of infestation over a threshold.

NETLOGO FEATURES

The draw-plants and draw-resistance procedures in this model use the primitive mouse-down? to check if the mouse button is pressed. It uses the mouse-xcor and mouse-ycor to figure out where the cursor is on the view.
